The student demonstrates partial understanding but makes a critical conceptual error by describing limits as "skipping math rules." This reveals a procedural approach without grasping that limits are the *foundation* upon which continuity and derivatives are built. The definition provided is circular and lacks the essential insight that limits describe behavior *near* a point, not necessarily *at* the point. The explanation of discontinuities conflates "function not existing" with limit behavior, which is imprecise.

**Assessment:** Below standard for AP Calculus AB. The student has an intuitive sense but lacks accurate conceptual grounding.

* Recognition that lim(x→c) f(x) can exist even when f(c) does not
* Understanding that limits describe function *behavior* rather than being a "workaround"

The response is superficial and would not receive full credit on an AP Calculus AB free-response question asking for an explanation of limits.

**Assessment:** Foundational awareness present, but insufficient depth for mastery-level understanding.

While oral exams permit more informal speech than written exams, the lack of mathematical structure and terminology suggests the student cannot articulate the concept with necessary precision even in conversational form.

**Assessment:** Adequate for basic communication but below AP standard for mathematical explanation.
